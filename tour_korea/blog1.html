Brief itinerary

  • Day 1: Jakarta- Incheon

Today, we will depart from Soekarno-Hatta International Airport to Korea.

  • Day 2: Incheon- Gimpo- Jeju-(L/D)

Upon arrival, you’ll enjoy the opportunity to learn the art of making Kimbap and dress in traditional Korean Hanbok. Later, you’ll head to the airport for a flight to Jeju Island. There, you’ll explore the famous Dragon Head Rock and experience the intriguing Mysterious Road, a 1.5 km stretch where gravity appears to defy the natural order, pulling objects uphill.

  • Day 3: Jeju Island-(Breakfast, lunch, and dinner)

Today, you will explore the wonders of Jeju Island, starting with a visit to Seongsan Sunrise Peak, a crater formed by a hydrovolcanic eruption in shallow seas around 5,000 years ago. Next, you’ll visit Seongeup Folk Village or Jeju Folk Village, a traditional village on Jeju Island. In the afternoon, the tour continues with stops at the Teddy Bear Museum and the Trick Eye & Ice Museum.

  • Day 4: Jeju- Gimpo- Nami Island- MT. Sorak (Breakfast, lunch, and dinner)

Today, you will be transferred to the airport for your flight back to Incheon. Upon arrival in Seoul, you will be taken to enjoy the beauty of Nami Island, the famous filming location of the Korean drama Winter Sonata. In the evening, you will rest in the Mt. Sorak area.

  • Day 5: MT. Sorak- Ski Resort- Seoul-(Breakfast and lunch)

After breakfast, you will visit Mt. Sorak National Park and take a cable car (weather permitting) to Gwomgeumsung Fortress, where you can also see the Grand Bronze Buddha at Shinheungsa Temple. Then, you will experience the fun of playing in the snow at a Ski Resort. The journey will continue to Seoul, where you will have the opportunity to shop at Dongdaemun Market.

  • Day 6: Seoul (Breakfast and lunch)

This morning, you’ll visit Gyeongbok Palace, the iconic filming location for the drama Kingdom (if it’s a Tuesday, Gyeongbok Palace is closed and will be replaced by Deoksu Palace), along with the National Folklore Museum and a drive-by of the Blue House, the official residence of South Korea’s president. In the afternoon, enjoy a visit to the Red Pine, Ginseng Outlet, and a Cosmetic Shop. In the evening, you’ll have free time to shop at the Duty-Free Shop and explore the lively Myeongdong Street.

  • Day 7: Seoul- Jakarta (Breakfast)

Today, you’ll visit a Korean Food and Souvenir Supermarket to pick up some local snacks and souvenirs (time permitting) before heading to the airport for your return flight. Thank you for being part of this tour, and we look forward to welcoming you on another one of our future programs.
