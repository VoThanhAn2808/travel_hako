Brief itinerary

  • Day 1: Arrive – Phnom Penh, Sour Sdey “Hello” Cambodia

Upon arrival at Phnom Penh Int Airport, process the immigration procedure (visa on arrival is available or obtain in advance via www.evisa.gov.kh), then get your luggage and walk straight to the Exit Gate where our representative is waiting. After self-introduced, you’ll transfer directly around 45 minutes to the hotel in the city for check in (check in time is 2pm). Time to fresh-up and relax at hotel. Overnight in Phnom Penh.

  • Day 2: Phnom Penh’s Past & Present ( Breakfast)

Begin with breakfast at your hotel. Visit the Royal Palace (built 1866), showcasing colorful Khmer architecture, and the nearby Silver Pagoda with Buddha statues adorned in diamonds, gold, and silver. Continue to the National Museum, a 1917 red Khmer-style building with over 5,000 artifacts. Next, see the Independence Monument, built in 1954 after Cambodia’s independence. Visit the Toul Sleng Genocide Museum (S21), a former Khmer Rouge prison offering insight into Cambodia’s history. At noon, explore the Central Market (Phsar Thmey), with its dome and endless stalls of goods. End the day at Wat Phnom, Phnom Penh's historic temple, popular for prayers and sunset views. Overnight in Phnom Penh.

  • Day 3: Phnom Penh – Siem Reap ( Breakfast)

After breakfast, departure from Phnom Penh to Siem Reap. Upon arrival, transfer to the hotel for check-in and the rest of the evening is free at your own time. Overnight in Siem Reap.

  • Day 4: Angkor Highlight ( Breakfast)

Start with breakfast at the hotel. Head to Angkor Thom, the fortress built by King Jayavarman VII after Angkor’s occupation by the Chams. Visit Bayon Temple at its center, a unique display of the king’s creativity. Stop by the Terrace of the Leper King, Terrace of Elephants, and Baphuon Temple, a stunning 11th-century structure. Explore Ta Prohm, a hauntingly beautiful temple overgrown with tree roots embracing its stones. Finally, visit Angkor Wat, the world’s largest religious building, a masterpiece of Khmer pride and architecture. Overnight in Siem Reap.

  • Day 5: Siem Reap – Departure (Breakfast)

Enjoy a boat ride on Tonle Sap Lake, Southeast Asia’s largest freshwater lake, which flows into the Tonle Sap River and connects with the Mekong in Phnom Penh. Visit the “floating village” with schools, a police station, and more, all floating and moving with the lake’s water levels. Then, visit ARTISAN D’ANGKOR, a project reviving ancient Khmer arts and crafts while supporting rural communities. Transfer to Siem Reap airport for your next destination.
