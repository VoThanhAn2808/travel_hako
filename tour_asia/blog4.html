Brief itinerary

  • DAY 01: Phnom Penh Arrival (Dinner)

Upon arrival at the airport, you will be transferred to your hotel for check-in. In the afternoon, you will visit the Royal Palace, which is currently the residence of His Majesty, the King of Cambodia, and the royal families. This palace is like a small town of royal dynasties. Afterward, you will continue to the Silver Pagoda, which is not only a functioning temple but also a repository for cultural treasures, including a 90 kg golden Buddha encrusted with 2086 diamonds. You will spend the night in Phnom Penh.

  • DAY 02: Phnom Penh-(Breakfast/Lunch/Dinner)

After breakfast at the hotel, drive to the Choeng Ek "Killing Fields," located 15 km south of Phnom Penh, where around 17,000 individuals were detained and transported for execution between 1975 and 1978. Visit the Independence Monument for photos and explore the Psa Thmey market. In the afternoon, tour the Tuol Sleng Museum (S-21 prison), a former school turned Khmer Rouge prison where thousands were held before being sent to Choeng Ek. Next, visit the Russian Market, which is famous for affordable clothing, Buddha images, woodcarvings, silk, and silver jewelry. Continue to Wat Phnom, a historic pagoda built in 1373 on a man-made hill by Daun Chi Penh. Return to the hotel after dinner or visit the Naga World Casino. Overnight in Phnom Penh.

  • DAY 03: Phnom Penh.– Siem Reap (B/L/D)

After breakfast at the hotel, transfer to the bus station or airport for your journey to Siem Reap. Upon arrival, you will be transferred to your hotel for check-in, followed by lunch at a local Indian restaurant. In the afternoon, visit the Floating Village and enjoy a ride on a traditional wooden boat on Tonle Sap Lake, experiencing the unique way of life with floating villages, stilted houses, fish traps, and a deep connection to the lake’s ecology. If time allows, visit Artisan D’Angkor, a center for wood and stone carving. Spend the night in Siem Reap.

  • DAY 04: Siem Reap (B/L/D)

Start your day with a visit to the South Gate of Angkor Thom, exploring Bayon Temple, The Royal Enclosure, Phimeanaka, and the Elephant and Leper King Terraces. Then, head to Ta Prohm, also known as the jungle temple. After lunch, visit Thommanan, Chaosay Tevoda, and the charming Ta Keo Temple, built around the same time as Angkor Wat. Next, explore Angkor Wat, one of the "7 Wonders of the World" and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Admire its splendor and the world’s longest bas-relief, depicting stories from Hindu mythology. End your day at Bakheng Hill for a stunning sunset view from the temple mountain. Spend the night in Siem Reap. Special note: The Bakan Tower at Angkor Wat is closed for four days each month for religious observances. Visitors should dress modestly (covering shoulders and knees) and wait in line for their turn to climb, as children under twelve are not allowed for safety reasons.

  • DAY 05: Siem Reap - Kulen National Park (B/L/D)

After breakfast, head to Kulen National Park on Kulen Mountain, the 9th-century capital of the Khmer Empire. Visit ancient temples, the River of 1,000 Lingas, a reclining Buddha, and a waterfall where you can cleanse with holy water. Return to town for a late lunch. In the afternoon, visit a silk farm in Pouk district, 15 km from Siem Reap, to see the silk production process. There’s also a shop for silk and souvenirs. On the way back, stop at the Western Baray, a large reservoir once used for irrigation. Overnight in Siem Reap.

  • DAY 06: Siem Reap - Departure (Breakfast)

Enjoy a complimentary and relaxed breakfast at the hotel before being transferred to the airport for your departure flight. Thank you for choosing our tour! We’re glad to have served you and hope you had a fantastic experience. See you soon!
